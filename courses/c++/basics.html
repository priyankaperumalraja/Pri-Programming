A Basic History of Computers

(So you know how current technology came to be).

Computer Science classes typically talk about the basic parts of a computer and the fundamentals of how they work, but often leave out the ancient history in which computational devices were formed.

At least to me, this led me to be very confused on how exactly people came up with the idea of the modern computer. I think that a basic understanding of the history of computers will help any Computer Science student succeed.

The Abacus.

The first ever recorded computer was the abacus in the 2000's BCE.

Although its exact location of origin in unknown, historical artifacts and information on the abacus have been found throughout ancient Europe and Asia.

https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/ Abacus_%28PSF%29.png/330px-Abacus_%28PSF%29.png

The abacus is typically made of a wood frame and rows of beads representing number places such as the ones, tens, hundreds places, and so on. Through the middle of the main frame is a wooden bar, typically with two beads on each row above representing the number 5, as well as five beads on each row below each representing the number 1.

Ancient people were able to use the abacusâ€™ system of place organization to keep track of very large numbers and conduct mathematical operations like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Addition was typically represented by moving beads away from the edges of the abacus to the center, while subtraction is moving beads away from the center. Multiplication and division involved multiple iterations of this process.

The abacus was used for millenia, and only until the 19th century CE did new ideas for computation emerge.
